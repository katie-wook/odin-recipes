Cornbread Dressing

Description

Cornbread is a family favorite around this time of year. This recipe involves making cornbread and then crumbling it into this dressing. You can make your cornbread from scratch or use a mix from the store - either way, this dish will be simple yet stunning this holiday season.

This recipe is best served fresh, but can be stored in an airtight container in the fridge for three to four days. You can reheat it in the microwave or the oven and can add broth as needed for additional moisture. Be careful not to bake for too long!

Ingredients

  • 3 cups crumbled cornbread
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1/2 cup chopped celery
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • 1 tablespoon dried sage, or more to taste
  • salt and ground black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease a 7x11-inch baking dish. Place crumbled cornbread in a large bowl.
  2. Melt butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Add onion and celery and sauté until soft, 5 to 7 minutes.
  3. Add sautéed onion and celery to the crumbled cornbread. Stir in chicken stock, eggs, sage, salt, and pepper until well combined. Pour dressing into the prepared baking dish.
  4. Bake in the preheated oven until dressing just starts to turn golden brown around the edges, about 30 minutes.
