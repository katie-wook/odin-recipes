Pumpkin Pie with Vanilla Meringue

Description

Pumpkin pie is a given for any Thanksgiving dessert selection, but that doesn't mean it can't be improved. Whipping up a light and fluffy vanilla meringue to top this delicious pie is sure to wow your Thanksgiving guests. Toasting the meringue with a chef's torch will give your pie some extra color and lovely marshmallow smell. This decadent treat will please in both taste and presentation this holiday season.

This pie will take a bit longer to make than the average pumpkin pie, since meringue is a step up from whipped cream. But most of the work can be handled by a stand mixer, leaving you to take a break or work on other menu items. This is still a relatively effortless recipe that's sure to wow, and as always, is best served fresh out of the oven.

Ingredients

Pumpkin Pie

  • 1 (9-inch) unbaked pie crust
  • 1 (15 ounce) can pumpkin puree
  • 1 (14 ounce) can sweetened condensed milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice

Vanilla Meringue

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 5 large egg whites
  • 1/4 teaspoon cream of tartar
  • 1 pinch kosher salt
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
